About Me

Hello.

I’m a graphic designer and Brand Marketing Specialist living in Taiwan. I’ve spent the last 3 years across the different surfaces of Content Marketing— including user research, UX and visual design, or even illustration. And applying these approaches to problems of all different shapes and sizes. Outside of work, you’ll find me telling stories through various creative mediums, whether it’s through painting or drawing, photography , and writing.

ABOUT

001:Beginning

Studied fine arts, self-taught design. I've always loved drawing or creating all along , but I never went to school for design.Don't know if it was necessary, either, but I definitely do not regret it.
It only took me one PS/AI class to get started. Most of my learning came from observation, browsing designer portfolios, reading about the design process, and so on.

What are some challenges I face when self-taught?

For having tried to learn a few things on my own, I would say that the main challenges when self-teaching are the following:

Maintaining self-discipline.

Maintaining motivation. When no one's pushing you, how do you motivate yourself? Without that discipline falls. One way I've found useful is not to have any negotiation with oneself.

Just do it.

Working without any "landmarks".

Sometimes, the biggest challenge will be the inability to tell how well you are doing or how poor your performance is.So I make a system for myselft in place to:
(1.)Measure my results and track my progress.
(2.)Reflect on what my really learned from my execution.(Implementation and experimentation)
(3.)Identify new problems and develop a new solution.
—then, repeat the process.

Maintaining the motivation.

Keep it interesting - amuse myself, define milestones, break the routine. This will make me motivation significantly, and heck, it keeps it fun :-)
